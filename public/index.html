Maybe this isn't a day at the beach afterall.

This project began as a deep dive into the benefits of sunscreen, with a viewpoint that sunscreen is empirically beneficial, and that everyone should wear it.
I quickly discovered that this viewpoint was centered on my own experience: a person with fair skin and a family history of skin cancer, and initially didn't take into account those with lived experiences different than my own.
The narrative that you just experienced mirrors my own journey in realizing how a seemingly necessary, protective, modern product is steeped in colonialism.
